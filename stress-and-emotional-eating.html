Stress and Emotional Eating

Published 2026-01-21 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Emotional eating refers to eating in response to feelings rather than actual physical hunger. Stress, boredom, sadness, and even excitement can all trigger the urge to eat, often leading people toward highly palatable comfort foods that are high in sugar and fat.

Why Stress Triggers Eating

Chronic stress raises cortisol levels, which can increase appetite and specifically drive cravings for calorie-dense foods. Additionally, eating can serve as a temporary distraction or source of comfort during emotionally difficult moments, reinforcing the behaviour over time.

Recognising Emotional Hunger

Physical hunger typically builds gradually and can be satisfied by a variety of foods, while emotional hunger often arrives suddenly, craves a specific comfort food, and does not go away even after eating enough to feel physically full.

Strategies to Manage Emotional Eating

  • Pause for a few minutes before eating to ask whether you are truly physically hungry
  • Keep a short list of non-food coping activities, such as a short walk or calling a friend
  • Avoid keeping large quantities of trigger foods easily accessible at home
  • Practice mindful eating by slowing down and paying attention to each bite

When to Seek Support

If emotional eating feels frequent, distressing, or difficult to control, speaking with a mental health professional or registered dietitian can provide personalised strategies and support beyond general tips.
