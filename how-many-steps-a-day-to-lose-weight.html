How Many Steps a Day to Lose Weight?

Published 2026-01-14 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

The popular goal of ten thousand steps a day did not originate from rigorous scientific research but from a marketing campaign in Japan decades ago. That said, it has become a reasonably useful benchmark, and research does show that increasing daily step count is strongly associated with better weight management and overall health.

How Steps Translate to Calories

On average, walking one kilometre burns roughly the same number of calories as half of your body weight in kilograms, meaning a person weighing seventy kilograms burns approximately thirty-five calories per kilometre walked. Over a full day, an extra few thousand steps can meaningfully add to your total calorie burn.

Realistic Targets Based on Current Activity

If you currently average around four thousand steps a day, jumping straight to ten thousand can feel overwhelming. A more sustainable approach is to increase your daily average by one to two thousand steps every couple of weeks until you reach a comfortable, consistent target.

Practical Ways to Add Steps

  • Take a short walk after each meal to aid digestion and add steps
  • Park further away or get off public transport one stop earlier
  • Take phone calls while walking instead of sitting

Steps Are Not the Whole Picture

While step count is a useful and motivating metric, combining walking with resistance training and a sensible calorie target will generally produce better long-term results than focusing on steps alone.
