How to Break a Weight Loss Plateau

Published 2026-01-18 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

A weight loss plateau occurs when the scale stops moving despite continuing to follow the same diet and exercise routine that previously produced steady results. This is an extremely common and entirely normal part of the weight loss process, not a sign of failure.

Why Plateaus Happen

As you lose weight, your body requires fewer calories to function, since a smaller body has lower energy needs. This means the same calorie intake that once created a deficit may now simply match your new, lower maintenance level, causing progress to stall.

Reassess Your Actual Intake

Over time, portion sizes can creep up gradually without much notice, and logging accuracy can slip. Revisiting your food diary with fresh attention to detail, including cooking oils and small snacks, often reveals extra calories that had gone unnoticed.

Adjust Your Targets

Recalculating your BMR and TDEE based on your current, lower body weight will often reveal that your calorie target needs a modest downward adjustment or that your activity level needs a slight increase.

Consider a Diet Break

Occasionally, taking a short one to two week break at maintenance calories can help reset hunger hormones and improve adherence when you resume your deficit, particularly after several months of continuous dieting.

Look Beyond the Scale

Measuring waist circumference, taking progress photos, and tracking how clothes fit can reveal continued fat loss even when the scale number temporarily holds steady.
