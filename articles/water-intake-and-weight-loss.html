Water Intake and Weight Loss

Published 2026-01-19 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Water does not contain any calories, yet it plays a surprisingly important supporting role in weight management, from regulating appetite to supporting the countless metabolic processes that keep your body functioning efficiently.

Water and Appetite

Thirst and hunger signals in the brain can sometimes be confused, leading people to eat when they are actually just dehydrated. Drinking a glass of water before meals has been shown in some studies to modestly reduce calorie intake by increasing feelings of fullness.

Water and Metabolic Function

Adequate hydration supports efficient digestion, nutrient transport, and the removal of metabolic waste products. Even mild dehydration has been linked in research to reduced energy levels and lower exercise performance, both of which can indirectly affect weight management efforts.

How Much Water Do You Need

A commonly cited general guideline is around eight glasses, or roughly two litres, per day, though actual needs vary based on body size, activity level, climate, and diet. A practical personal indicator is checking that urine remains pale yellow throughout the day.

Simple Ways to Increase Water Intake

  • Keep a water bottle visible on your desk or nearby at all times
  • Drink a glass of water immediately after waking up
  • Replace at least one sugary drink per day with plain or infused water
