Ramadan Fasting and Weight Management

Published 2026-01-30 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Ramadan brings a significant shift in eating patterns, with extended daily fasting periods followed by meals at suhoor before dawn and iftar after sunset. With some thoughtful planning, it is entirely possible to maintain or even improve your weight management goals during this month.

Building a Balanced Suhoor

A suhoor meal rich in protein, fibre, and healthy fats digests more slowly, helping you feel fuller for longer during the fasting hours. Options such as eggs, yogurt, oats, and whole grain roti are generally better choices than heavily fried or sugary foods.

Breaking Your Fast Wisely

It is tempting to overeat quickly at iftar after a long day of fasting, but starting with dates and water, followed by a modest portion of soup or fruit, allows your digestive system to adjust before moving on to the main meal.

Watch Portion Sizes at the Main Meal

Traditional iftar spreads often include fried items like pakoray and samosay alongside heavier dishes. Choosing smaller portions of fried snacks and prioritising grilled or baked options can significantly reduce total calorie intake without sacrificing enjoyment.

Stay Hydrated Between Iftar and Suhoor

Spreading water intake across the evening and night, rather than drinking large amounts all at once, helps maintain proper hydration throughout the following fasting day.

Keep Moving

Light activity such as a walk after iftar, once digestion has settled, can support energy levels and overall weight management goals throughout the month.
