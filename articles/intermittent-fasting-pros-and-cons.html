Intermittent Fasting: Pros and Cons

Published 2026-01-17 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Intermittent fasting refers to eating patterns that cycle between periods of eating and periods of fasting, rather than focusing on which specific foods to eat. The most common approach, often called 16:8, involves eating within an eight-hour window and fasting for the remaining sixteen hours of the day.

Potential Benefits

For many people, restricting eating to a shorter window naturally reduces overall calorie intake, since there is simply less time available for snacking and extra meals. Some people also find that fasting simplifies decision-making around food, which can reduce mental fatigue related to constant meal planning.

Potential Drawbacks

Intermittent fasting is not inherently magical, and its benefits largely come down to the same calorie deficit principle as any other diet approach. Some people experience irritability, difficulty concentrating, or disrupted sleep during the fasting window, particularly in the first few weeks of adjustment.

Who Should Be Cautious

People with a history of disordered eating, pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with certain medical conditions such as diabetes should consult a doctor before attempting intermittent fasting, since prolonged fasting periods can carry additional risks for these groups.

Is It Right for You

Intermittent fasting is simply one tool among many. If the eating pattern fits comfortably into your lifestyle and helps you maintain a calorie deficit without excessive hunger or stress, it can be a reasonable option. If it feels miserable or unsustainable, a more traditional eating schedule may serve you better.
