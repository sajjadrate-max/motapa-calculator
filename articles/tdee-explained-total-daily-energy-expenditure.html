TDEE Explained: Total Daily Energy Expenditure

Published 2026-01-07 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Total Daily Energy Expenditure, abbreviated as TDEE, represents the total number of calories your body burns in a full 24-hour period. Unlike BMR, which only measures calories burned at rest, TDEE adds in everything else: walking, exercising, digesting food, fidgeting, and even the act of thinking.

The Components of TDEE

TDEE is generally broken into four parts. The first and largest is your basal metabolic rate. The second is the thermic effect of food, which is the energy used to digest and process what you eat. The third is exercise activity, covering deliberate workouts. The fourth, often underestimated, is non-exercise activity thermogenesis, which includes daily movements like walking around the house, standing, and even talking with your hands.

Calculating TDEE

The simplest way to estimate TDEE is to multiply your BMR by an activity multiplier that reflects your typical week. A sedentary desk worker might use a multiplier around 1.2, while someone who exercises intensely six days a week could use a multiplier closer to 1.7 or higher.

Using TDEE for Your Goals

Once you know your TDEE, weight management becomes a matter of simple math. Eating below your TDEE creates a calorie deficit that leads to fat loss over time, while eating above it creates a surplus that supports weight or muscle gain. Eating at roughly your TDEE helps you maintain your current weight.

Adjusting Over Time

TDEE is not fixed. As you lose weight, your body generally needs fewer calories to maintain itself, so it is wise to recalculate every few weeks and adjust your targets so that progress continues steadily.
