How to Read Food Labels

Published 2026-01-22 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Nutrition labels contain a wealth of useful information, but they can feel confusing without knowing exactly what to look for. Learning to read them quickly and accurately is one of the most valuable skills for anyone managing their weight or overall health.

Start With the Serving Size

All the numbers on a nutrition label are based on a specific serving size, which may be smaller than what you would naturally eat in one sitting. Always check the serving size first, since eating double the listed serving means doubling every number on the label.

Focus on Calories and Macronutrients

The total calories per serving give you a quick sense of how energy-dense the food is, while the breakdown of protein, carbohydrates, and fat helps you understand how that food fits into your daily targets.

Check Added Sugar and Sodium

Added sugar, listed separately from total sugar on many labels, is worth watching closely since it contributes calories without meaningful nutritional value. Sodium levels are also important to monitor, particularly for anyone managing blood pressure.

Read the Ingredient List

Ingredients are listed in descending order by weight, meaning the first few ingredients make up the bulk of the product. A shorter, more recognisable ingredient list generally indicates a less processed food.

Putting It All Together

Comparing labels across similar products, such as two brands of yogurt or bread, can help you consistently choose the option that better fits your calorie and nutrition goals.
