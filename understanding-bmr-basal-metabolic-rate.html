Understanding BMR: Calories Your Body Burns at Rest

Published 2026-01-06 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Basal Metabolic Rate, or BMR, is the number of calories your body needs simply to stay alive while completely at rest. This includes keeping your heart beating, your lungs breathing, your brain functioning, and your cells repairing themselves. For most people, BMR accounts for the largest single share of total daily calorie burn, often between 60 and 75 percent.

What Influences Your BMR

Several factors affect how many calories your body burns at rest, including your age, sex, weight, height, and amount of lean muscle tissue. Muscle is metabolically active tissue, so people with more muscle mass generally have a higher BMR than people with a higher percentage of body fat, even at the same total body weight.

How BMR Is Estimated

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is one of the most widely used and accurate formulas for estimating BMR without expensive lab equipment. It uses your weight, height, age, and sex to produce a reasonably close estimate. While direct lab testing using indirect calorimetry is more precise, formula-based estimates work well for everyday planning.

Why BMR Matters for Weight Management

Once you know your BMR, you can build on it to estimate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure by factoring in your activity level. This combined number tells you roughly how many calories you burn in an average day, which is the starting point for creating a calorie deficit for weight loss or a surplus for muscle gain.

Common Mistakes

A common mistake is eating far below BMR for extended periods, which can slow metabolism, cause muscle loss, and lead to nutrient deficiencies. A sustainable plan respects your BMR as a floor rather than a target to undercut aggressively.
