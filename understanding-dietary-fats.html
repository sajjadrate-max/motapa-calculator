Understanding Dietary Fats

Published 2026-01-11 • Motapa Calculator Health Guides

Dietary fat has long been misunderstood, often blamed for weight gain simply because it contains more calories per gram than protein or carbohydrates. In reality, fat is an essential nutrient required for hormone production, vitamin absorption, and cell structure.

Types of Dietary Fat

Unsaturated fats, found in olive oil, nuts, seeds, and fatty fish, are generally considered heart-healthy and are linked to improved cholesterol profiles. Saturated fats, found in butter, ghee, and fatty meats, are not inherently harmful in moderation but are best kept to a reasonable portion of total calories. Trans fats, often found in heavily processed and fried packaged foods, are the type most consistently linked to negative health outcomes and are worth minimizing wherever possible.

How Much Fat Do You Need

Most dietary guidelines suggest that fat should make up roughly 20 to 35 percent of total daily calories, though individual needs can vary based on activity level and personal health goals.

Fat and Satiety

Fat slows digestion and adds richness to meals, which can help increase feelings of fullness and satisfaction, making it easier to stick to a calorie-controlled eating plan without feeling deprived.

Smart Fat Choices

  • Cook with olive oil or a modest amount of ghee instead of heavily processed vegetable oils
  • Include a handful of nuts or seeds as a daily snack
  • Eat oily fish such as salmon or sardines a couple of times a week where possible
